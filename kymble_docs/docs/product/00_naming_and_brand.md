# Naming and Brand — Kymble

## Selected name

**Kymble**

Pronunciation: **KIM-buhl**

The name combines **Kylon** and **Nimble** without sounding like a generic AI product. It is short, slightly playful, easy to say in a three-minute demo, and directly honors the two sponsor products at the center of the build.

## Why this name works

- It sounds like a real product rather than a generated phrase such as “Signal Intelligence Operating System.”
- The sponsor connection is visible once explained: **KY** from Kylon + **MBLE** from Nimble.
- It can be used as a noun or a verb: “Send this account to Kymble” or “Kymble this customer story.”
- It supports a warm, operator-focused identity instead of a robotic one.

## Tagline

> **Kymble finds who needs you next—and shows the receipts.**

Alternative short tagline for the demo screen:

> **Customer proof in. Qualified pipeline out.**

## One-sentence description

Kymble is an AI GTM organization built for Nimble and Kylon that learns from real customer wins, finds similar buying conditions on the live web, and coordinates evidence-backed pipeline actions with human approval.

## Brand personality

Kymble should feel:

- curious rather than omniscient;
- operational rather than conversational;
- playful but trustworthy;
- evidence-first rather than hype-first;
- visibly collaborative rather than “one magical agent.”

## Copy rules

Use:

- “AI coworkers” or “GTM team” in user-facing copy;
- “evidence,” “source,” “why now,” and “next action” prominently;
- “verified,” “inferred,” “synthetic,” and “cached” labels consistently;
- short, human status messages such as “Scout found 3 sources” rather than model-centric phrases.

Avoid:

- “revolutionary,” “10x,” “autonomous everything,” or “fully replaces your sales team”;
- names ending in “AI,” “OS,” “Copilot,” or “GPT” in the product title;
- implying that Kymble knows private facts from public evidence;
- calling synthetic demo records real.

## Other names considered

- **Nilon** — funny and memorable, but easily confused with nylon fabric.
- **Nylon** — strongest joke, weakest product searchability.
- **Nymble** — close to Nimble and therefore less distinct.
- **Kynim** — compact but harder to pronounce.
- **Kylonble** — explicit but awkward.

Kymble is the recommended balance of sponsor connection, memorability, and product credibility.
