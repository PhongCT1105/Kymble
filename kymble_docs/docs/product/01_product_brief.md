# Product Brief — Kymble

## Product

**Kymble** — the AI GTM organization for Nimble and Kylon.

> **Kymble finds who needs you next—and shows the receipts.**

## Challenge fit

Kymble is built first for the **Nimble & Kylon GTM AI Employee Challenge**. It also fits the hackathon theme “Build your AI organization” and can be submitted under Track 3 as the general category.

The product should be judged as a sponsor-specific operating system for finding the next Nimble and Kylon customers, not as a generic lead-generation app.

## Primary users

### During the hackathon

- Nimble GTM, partnerships, and developer-relations teams;
- Kylon founders and early GTM operators;
- judges evaluating whether the project uses both sponsor products deeply.

### Future users

A B2B founder or GTM leader with real customer stories who wants to convert those stories into repeatable, evidence-backed pipeline.

## Problem

Customer case studies contain the strongest available clues about demand:

- what the customer was trying to build;
- what broke or became expensive;
- what event created urgency;
- what capability mattered;
- what changed after purchase.

Most GTM systems ignore that information. They begin with broad firmographics, create a large list, and ask AI to personalize messages. This scales outreach before proving why the account should be contacted.

Nimble and Kylon face a related challenge:

- Nimble needs to identify companies whose products are becoming dependent on fresh, structured, reliable web intelligence.
- Kylon needs to identify companies whose growing AI workforce creates shared-context, coordination, governance, and human-approval problems.
- The joint opportunity is a company building several AI agents that require live web intelligence and must collaborate with a human organization.

## Solution

Kymble deploys a small AI GTM team that:

1. reads a verified Nimble customer case study;
2. extracts a structured customer DNA profile;
3. maps private customer pain to observable public buying signals;
4. uses Nimble to discover and extract current evidence;
5. verifies source support, dates, and independence;
6. calculates separate Nimble and Kylon fit scores;
7. writes all records into a lightweight CRM;
8. coordinates the mission and approval flow through Kylon;
9. recommends a useful next action for a human to approve.

## Why the sponsors are essential

### Nimble is the intelligence layer

Nimble is used for the hardest external work:

- discovering candidate companies;
- finding product launches, engineering posts, documentation, and job triggers;
- extracting page-level evidence rather than relying on snippets;
- returning structured facts with URLs and timestamps;
- refreshing signals when the public web changes.

Without Nimble, Kymble loses its live demand-detection capability.

### Kylon is the organization layer

Kylon is where the AI coworkers and human operator share:

- the mission goal;
- the customer pattern;
- candidate accounts;
- evidence and structured records;
- ownership and handoffs;
- approval requests;
- the final decision.

Without Kylon, Kymble becomes a background research script rather than an AI organization.

## Primary demo mission

> Learn from Alta’s real Nimble customer story. Find AI companies that may need Nimble for reliable live-web intelligence and Kylon for coordinating the human-agent team that acts on it.

## Supporting customer patterns

- **Alta-like:** agentic GTM product needing deep, structured prospect intelligence at scale.
- **Qodo-like:** AI product harmed by stale technical context.
- **Grips-like:** data product requiring high-volume, normalized web extraction.
- **Kylon-like hypothesis:** AI-native company with multiple agents, fragmented context, and human-approval requirements.

## Product principles

### Customer proof before ICP

Begin with a real closed-won story, not an invented buyer persona.

### Evidence before copy

No outreach recommendation should exist until the account has supported evidence.

### Events before firmographics

Recent launches, hiring, product changes, and workflow statements matter more than static industry and headcount.

### State changes before prose

The demo must create records, attach evidence, update scores, move stages, assign ownership, and request approval.

### Human authority

Research can be autonomous. External action requires an explicit human decision.

### Honesty by construction

Verified, inferred, synthetic, and cached data must be visually distinct.

## Main user story

As a Nimble or Kylon GTM leader, I want Kymble to learn from a successful customer, search for companies entering the same buying situation, and present a source-backed next action so I can prioritize the right account without manually researching hundreds of companies.

## Success criteria

Judges should understand within three minutes that:

1. Kymble solves a real sponsor GTM problem.
2. Nimble powers live public-web discovery and extraction.
3. Kylon powers the AI organization and approval workflow.
4. The system creates operational CRM state, not only text.
5. Every account recommendation can be audited back to evidence.
6. A human remains responsible for sensitive actions.
7. The same engine can later learn from other B2B customer stories.

## MVP outcome

The successful demo produces:

- one verified customer DNA profile;
- a sponsor-specific signal blueprint;
- one live or cached researched account;
- at least two verified evidence items;
- separate Nimble and Kylon scores;
- a recommended joint pilot or sponsor-specific play;
- a Kylon approval request;
- a human-approved, clearly simulated opportunity update.
